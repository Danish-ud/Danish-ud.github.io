Overview

Telecom companies lose a significant portion of their revenue due to customer churn. The main aim of this project is to find the reasosn why customers are leaving the telecom companies. Find out all the reasons affecting customer churn and recommend business solutions to tackle those problems.

  • Identify key factors that lead to customer churn
  • Understand patterns and behaviors of churned customers
  • Provide actionable insights to reduce churn
  • Build an interactive Tableau dashboard to present findings

    • Tables

    The dataset contains 4 interrelated tables:

  • customers:- Demographic information of customers
  • services :- Details of services subscribed.
  • usage :-Monthly data usage and minutes used.
  • churn :-Customer churn status and billing details.

    • Recommendations

    Encourage Long-Term Contracts

  • Customers on month-to-month plans exhibit significantly higher churn. Recommendation Offer discounts, cashback, or exclusive features for customers switching to 1-year or 2-year contracts, and Introduce auto-renewal benefits or loyalty points for long-term users.

    • Preemptive Retention During High-Risk Months

  • Churn peaks during December and January. Recommend Launch targeted retention campaigns during Q4 and early Q1, and Provide holiday or new year incentives, including data bonuses, plan upgrades, or limited-time offers

    • Address Underutilization of Services

  • Histogram plots reveal many customers are not using their full GB or call allowances. I Recommend Segment and offer personalized, lower-tier plans to low-usage users and, Educate customers about plan benefits to increase engagement and satisfaction

    • Focus on Senior Citizens & Partnered Customers

  • Churn is slightly higher among senior citizens and customers with partners. I Recommend Design senior-friendly plans with simplified services and exclusive support and, Introduce family or couple plans to boost retention among partnered users.

    • Understand and Act on Gender-Based Trends

  • Churn rate is higher among male customers.I Recommend Conduct customer interviews or surveys to identify potential service gaps and, Run gender-focused marketing or engagement initiatives to address specific concerns.
